Understanding Jig Saw Specifications

Max Cutting Capacity – Wood (mm)

This specifies the thickest piece of wood the tool can cut effectively in a single pass. Staying within this limit ensures smooth cuts, prevents excessive wear on the blade, and reduces strain on the motor, helping maintain consistent performance.

Max Cutting Capacity – Steel (mm)

This indicates the maximum thickness of steel the tool can cut safely. Operating within this range prevents blade damage, reduces motor overload, and ensures accurate cuts in tougher metal materials.

Max Cutting Capacity – Aluminium (mm)

This shows the largest aluminium section the saw can cut efficiently. Following this limit helps avoid material deformation, ensures clean edges, and extends both blade and tool lifespan.

Vibration Emission Value (m/s²)

This value measures the vibration transmitted to the operator’s hands during cutting. Lower vibration improves comfort, reduces fatigue during extended use, and provides better control for precise and safe cutting operations.

Stroke Length (mm)

Represents the distance the saw blade travels in one complete stroke. Longer strokes allow faster and more aggressive cutting, while shorter strokes provide better control for precise work.
