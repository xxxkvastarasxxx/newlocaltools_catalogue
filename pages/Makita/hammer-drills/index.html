Understanding SDS Hammer and Scraper Specs

Impact Energy (J)

Higher impact energy means faster progress in concrete or masonry. Light bodies (around 1.2 J) suit anchors and overhead work; mid-tier (2.0-2.5 J) balance speed and control; heavy AVT models near 3 J tackle larger holes and chiselling.

RPM and BPM

RPM sets drilling speed while BPM measures hammer blows. Use higher RPM/BPM for small bits in softer material; drop speed for larger diameters to keep bits cool and maintain straight holes.

Max Drilling Capacity

The concrete, wood, and steel limits show the largest recommended diameter for each material. Staying within these ranges protects the motor, keeps runtime high, and extends bit life.

Weight and Length

Shorter, lighter tools are ideal for overhead anchors and tight spots; longer, heavier dual-18V or AVT bodies deliver more energy with better vibration damping for continuous drilling or chiselling.
