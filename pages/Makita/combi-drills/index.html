Understanding Combi Drill Specifications

RPM (Revolutions Per Minute)

RPM indicates the rotational speed of the drill chuck. Variable speed control allows lower speeds for accurate, controlled drilling and higher speeds for fast, efficient drilling in softer materials such as timber.

BPM (Beats Per Minute)

BPM refers to the number of hammer impacts delivered per minute when operating in percussion mode. Higher BPM values improve drilling performance in masonry and other dense substrates.

Torque (Nm)

Torque measures the rotational force produced by the motor. Higher torque enables the drill to drive fasteners efficiently and maintain performance under load, particularly when working with hardwoods or larger screws.

Maximum Drilling Capacity Metal

This specification defines the maximum diameter the drill can achieve in metal using a suitable twist drill bit. Adhering to this limit ensures optimal performance when drilling materials such as steel or aluminium and helps prevent motor overload and premature bit wear.

Maximum Drilling Capacity Wood

The wood drilling capacity specifies the largest bit diameter the tool can drive through softwood or hardwood without excessive strain. Models with higher capacities are better suited for carpentry tasks involving large spade bits, auger bits, or hole saws.
